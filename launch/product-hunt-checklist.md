# Product Hunt — launch-day checklist

*Do PH 3–5 days AFTER your Hacker News launch, once you have GitHub stars + ideally a testimonial. PH runs on Pacific Time; a launch day is 12:01am PT → 11:59pm PT.*

---

## T-minus 3–5 days (prep)
- [ ] **PH account ready** (yours). A little history/activity looks better than a same-day account — comment on a few products beforehand.
- [ ] **Pick the date:** a **Tuesday or Wednesday** you can be online most of the day to reply. Avoid Fri/weekends/holidays.
- [ ] **Draft the listing** (copy is in the "Product Hunt" doc):
  - [ ] Name: **LeakTrap**
  - [ ] Tagline (≤60 char): *Scan your AI-built app for leaks before you ship — in plain English*
  - [ ] Topics: Developer Tools, Security, Artificial Intelligence, No-Code, SaaS
  - [ ] Links: leaktrap.dev + github.com/Goatnuts1/leaktrap
- [ ] **Gallery assets** (this is what people actually look at):
  - [ ] The **8-second terminal GIF/MP4** (the red "Do not ship" scan) — the hero
  - [ ] The **8-checks** table/graphic
  - [ ] A before/after "fix" of one finding
  - [ ] Logo/thumbnail = the shield
- [ ] **Write your maker's first comment** (the story — drafted). Update the stat to the latest corpus number.
- [ ] **Line up your amplifiers:** waitlist (Loops), X followers, the Discords/subreddits you seeded on HN day. Have the notify-copy ready.

## T-minus 1 day
- [ ] **Schedule the launch** in PH for **12:01am PT** on your date (PH lets you schedule — it goes live automatically; you don't have to be awake).
- [ ] Double-check every gallery asset renders + every link works.
- [ ] Refresh the corpus stat if it's drifted; update the first comment.
- [ ] Get a good night's sleep — launch day is a marathon of replies.

## Launch day — morning (when you wake up, ~6–8am PT / 9–11am ET)
- [ ] Confirm it went live at 12:01am PT and looks right.
- [ ] **Post your maker's first comment** immediately (the story + what it is + `npx leaktrap`).
- [ ] **Notify your network** — but never say "please upvote" (against PH rules + looks desperate). Say *"We're live on Product Hunt today — would love your feedback 🎉 [link]"*:
  - [ ] Email the **Loops waitlist**
  - [ ] Post from **@ChrisinSRB** (X) + the brand account
  - [ ] Drop in the **Discords/subreddits** you already seeded (as a "we're live" update, not a fresh pitch)
- [ ] Pin the PH link to your X profile for the day.

## Launch day — throughout
- [ ] **Reply to EVERY comment** within minutes for the first few hours — engagement velocity feeds the ranking.
- [ ] Answer the expected questions: *"how's this different from Snyk?"* (built for founders, 10-sec answer) and *"how do you know it's the service_role key?"* (you decode the JWT).
- [ ] Thank people genuinely; ask commenters what checks they'd want next (turns comments into a thread).
- [ ] Post a mid-day X update if it's going well ("we're #X on PH right now 🙏").
- [ ] Keep checking through the evening — PT time zone means late US activity still counts.

## Do NOT
- ❌ Ask directly for upvotes (PH can penalize you; also reads badly).
- ❌ Use vote-exchange groups / buy votes — the algorithm catches it and can disqualify you.
- ❌ Copy-paste the same notify message everywhere at once — vary it.

## After (next day+)
- [ ] Add the **"Featured on Product Hunt" badge** to leaktrap.dev (evergreen social proof).
- [ ] Thank everyone who supported (a quick X post).
- [ ] Follow up with new waitlist signups.
- [ ] Note the result in your ledger; fold any good feedback into the roadmap issue.

---

## Honest expectation
Solo dev, modest network → **don't bank on #1 Product of the Day** (those are usually well-networked launches). A solid run = a few hundred visitors, some signups, maybe a top-10 badge, and lasting "featured on PH" credibility. It's an **amplifier**, not the main event — **HN + the X thread are your priority**; PH is the follow-up wave.
